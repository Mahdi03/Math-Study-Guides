  • Exponential Growth:
  • Most of the time the formula will end up bogging down to
    • y = Cekt
  • Where C is your initial value (when t = 0).
  • Where k is the proportionality constant that tells by just how much will the values grow or decay.
  • Where t represents time, in whatever unit required by certain problems.
  • This formula can actually be "anti-derived" by the formula given below, which also explains why k is a "proportionality" constant, because the Rate of Change varies directly or proportionally.
