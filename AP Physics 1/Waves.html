Simple Harmonic Motion:

  • Objects such as springs and pendulums do not move in a linear path, instead, they move in a back and forth path continuously, they both exhibit forms of Simple Harmonic Motion.
  • All this is, is when an object oscillates back and forth uninterrupted as the system tries to restore itself to equilibrium (the center point where the system no longer moves, or when displacement is 0), such that when its motion is graphed on a line, it produces a sinusoidal function (it moves like a sine graph).
  • These objects oscillate a repeated amount of times, and so the length of each oscillation (how long it takes for the object to repeat its movement) is known at the period ( T), and the amount of times this oscillation repeats in one second is known as Frequency ( f).

    • Springs:
  • Springs have a Spring Force Constant ( k) that varies from spring to spring.
  • When you stretch a spring, as it tries to bring itself back to equilibrium, the Restoring Force is given by Hooke's Law:
    • Fs = −kx
  • The (−) is there to tell you that the force is in the opposite direction of the displacement, and the formula tells you that displacement is proportional to the Restoring Force.
