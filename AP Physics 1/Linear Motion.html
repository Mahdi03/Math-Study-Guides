Graphical Representations of Position, Velocity, and Acceleration:
  • Average Rate of Change, or AROC, is the rate of change in between two points on a graph, whereas Instantaneous Rate of Change, IROC, is the rate of change at a single point on the graph. If you've taken calculus, this is also known as the derivative.
  • Velocity is the rate of change in a displacement versus time graph.
  • Acceleration is the rate of change in a velocity versus time graph.
  • The area under the curve refers to the area in between the graph and the x-axis. If it is below the x-axis then the area is negative. If you've taken calculus, this is known as the integral.
  • The area under the curve of an acceleration versus time graph is the velocity.
  • The area under the curve of a velocity versus time graph is the displacement.
  • The area under the graph can be represented by integrals if you've taken calculus or are concurrently enrolled.
