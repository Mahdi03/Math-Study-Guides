    Torque:
  • Torque is a rotational force on a rotating object.
  • We take into account the r because as r increases, the torque on the object increases, and the θ is there to take into account only the part of the force that is making the object rotate.
  • Just as force ( F) applied for some time ( t) will change an object's linear momentum ( p), torque ( τ) applied over a certain time ( t) will change the object's angular momentum ( L).
  • Angular Momentum ( L) is conserved just as Linear Momentum ( p), when no Net Force ( Fnet) (or in this case Net Torque ( τnet)) is applied.
  • The second formula for Angular Momentum L = mVr applies to point-masses in a rotational collision, where r is the distance from the axis of rotation to the line of the path of the point-mass and V is the linear velocity of the object.
