Prerequisites:

  • Be able to solve for variables in an equation or multiple equations.
  • Know algebra.
  • Recall trigonometry.

    • Vectors:

  • Many of the values in this course are represented with vectors: distance, speed, acceleration, forces, really any type of movement.
  • Vectors are a value (a magnitude) and a direction (12 ft East).
  • Scalars are numbers that do not have a direction (12 lbs).
  • Distance is how much the object traveled, it's scalar.
  • Displacement (Δx) directly from start to finish, it's a vector.
  • Speed is how quickly the object is covering distance.
  • Average speed is
    distance
    t
  • Velocity is how quickly it's changing position in a certain direction.
  • Average velocity is
    Δx
    Δt
    where Δ simply means "change in," so Δx is equal to your final position minus your initial position, or
    (xf − xi)
  • Vectors can be broken apart into x-direction and y-direction components, like a right triangle. It is helpful to do this when adding and subtracting vectors.
    • Parallel Vectors:
  • Vectors in the same direction add together or reinforce.
  • Vectors in the opposite direction subtract from each other, or cancel out.
    • Perpendicular Vectors:
  • Just use the Pythagorean Theorem after breaking them up into x and y components to get the complete vector.

    • Dimensional Analysis:

  • Another useful trick for converting units is dimensional analysis, simply it is converting one value such as yards per second into another value such as miles per hour.
  • The goal of dimensional analysis is to cancel out all of the units that you don't need to end up with the final result.

Linear Motion:

    Graphical Representations of Position, Velocity, and Acceleration:
  • Average Rate of Change, or AROC, is the rate of change in between two points on a graph, whereas Instantaneous Rate of Change, IROC, is the rate of change at a single point on the graph. If you've taken calculus, this is also known as the derivative.
  • Velocity is the rate of change in a displacement versus time graph.
  • Acceleration is the rate of change in a velocity versus time graph.
  • The area under the curve refers to the area in bewteen the graph and the x-axis. If it is below the x-axis then the area is negative. If you've taken calculus, this is known as the integral.
  • The area under the curve of an acceleration versus time graph is the velocity.
  • The area under the curve of a velocity versus time graph is the displacement.
  • As previously stated, velocity (V) is
    Δx
    Δt
    , and acceleration (a) is
    ΔV
    Δt
