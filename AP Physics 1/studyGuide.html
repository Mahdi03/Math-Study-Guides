Prerequisites:

  • Be able to solve for variables in an equation or multiple equations.
  • Know algebra.
  • Recall trigonometry.

    • Vectors:

  • Many of the values in this course are represented with vectors: distance, speed, acceleration, forces, really any type of movement.
  • Vectors are a value (a magnitude) and a direction (12 ft East).
  • Scalars are numbers that do not have a direction (12 lbs).
  • Distance is how much the object traveled, it's scalar.
  • Displacement (Δx) directly from start to finish, it's a vector.
  • Speed is how quickly the object is covering distance.
  • Average speed is
    distance
    t
  • Velocity is how quickly it's changing position in a certain direction.
  • Average velocity is
    Δx
    t
  • Vectors can be broken apart into x-direction and y-direction components, like a right triangle. It is helpful to do this when adding and subtracting vectors.
    • Parallel Vectors:
  • Vectors in the same direction add together or reinforce.
  • Vectors in the opposite direction subtract from each other, or cancel out.
    • Perpindicular Vectors:
  • Just use the Pythagorean Theorem after breaking them up into x and y components to get the complete vector.
