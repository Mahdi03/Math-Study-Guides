Lewis Structures:

  • Here's some guideline steps on how to draw Lewis Structures:
    1. Find the Total Number of Valence Electrons
    2. Find the Number of Electrons Needed to Make the Atoms "Happy" (complete the octet rule)
    3. Determine the Number of Bonds in the Molecule.
    4. Choose a Central Atom.
    5. Draw a Skeletal Structure.
    6. Place Electrons Around Outside Atoms.

Formal Charge:

  • With Lewis Structures we like to calculate the charge of each element if they all equally shared electrons. We can calculate the formal charge of each element in a Lewis Structure in many ways:
  • Smaller former charges are preferable to larger charges, however, most preferred would be that all atoms have a formal charge of 0
  • We like to avoid like charges on adjacent atoms
  • A more negative formal charge should be on the most electronegative atom

VSEPR and Molecular Geometry:

  • VSEPR stands for Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion, and when we draw Lewis Structures we only take into account the valence electrons.
  • VSEPR says that electrons exist in pairs and these pairs repel each other to be as far apart as possible. We call these electron domains, and that's the same as the steric number. Basically just count how many things are attached to the central atom(s).
