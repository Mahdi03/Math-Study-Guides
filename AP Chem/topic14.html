Endothermic and Exothermic Processes:

    Endothermic:
  • In Endothermic Reactions, energy (in the form of heat) is transferred from the surroundings into the system
  • The reaction feels cold to the touch, and the thermometer will pick up a decrease in temperature; so heat is absorbed.
  • Liquid to Gas and Solid to Liquid phase changes are all endothermic processes.
  • Products are at a higher energy level than the reactants
    • Exothermic:
  • In Exothermic Reactions, energy (in the form of heat) is transferred from the system out to its surroundings
  • The reaction feels warm to the touch, and the thermometer will pick up an increase in temperature; so heat is released.
  • Products are at a lower energy level than the reactants

Energy, Enthalpy, and Heat:

  • Energy is the capacity to do work or transfer heat
  • Whenever there is an energy difference, heat will transfer from the hotter areas to the colder areas, this is given by the 2nd Law of Thermodynamics
  • Enthalpy is used to measure changes in energy for a constant pressure system
  • If δH is positive, it's an endothermic process
  • If δH is negative, it's an exothermic process

Heat Transfer and Thermal Equilibrium:

  • This heat transfer occurs until the two items or mediums have reached a thermal equilibrium, remember that in thermal equilibrium there is the most randomness in the assortment of particles, this is also known as entropy.
