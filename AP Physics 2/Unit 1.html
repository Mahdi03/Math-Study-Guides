General Relativity:

  • Acceleration is accompanied by forces, the observer that is accelerating feels the time dilation and the length contraction.
  • While Special Relativity deals with differences in velocities, it's all inertial. General Relativity deals with acceleration.
  • Accelerating feels like an external gravitational field, and all physics falls under this equivalence principle.
  • If you accelerate at a relativistic rate, a light beam will curve as if it were undergoing projectile motion. Therefore we can deduce that gravtiy makes light curve (this effect is known as Gravitational Lensing)

    The 4 Fundamental Forces:
  • We would assume gravity is a fundamental force but in reality it is actually a part of the following force
    1. Electromagnetism - Attraction and repulsion based on charge, it's the force that allows you to push objects and stand, it's the microscopic repulsion.
    2. Strong nuclear force - The force that holds protons and neutrons together
    3. Weak nuclear force = Allows quarks to change their "flavor", allows protons → neutrons and vice versa
  • In a nucleus, you have all three forces working together and against each other, it's really chaotic since the protons are pushing away from each other but the strong force holds them together
  • When a nucleus is formed, they emit a binding energy which results in a mass defect, meaning that the before products have a higher mass than the final products. Using Eintein's Mass-Energy formula with the mass difference, we can calculate how much binding energy was released from a nuclear equation and a Periodic Table of Elements.
