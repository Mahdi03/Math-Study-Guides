    • Bernoulli's Equation:
  • When the velocity increases, that energy change has to be coming from somewhere. Either there has to be a difference in potential energies or pressures. A pressure difference (due to difference cross-sectional areas) means there is a net force doing work on the fluid, increasing the kinetic energy. Similarly, as velocity increases (↑), pressure drops (↓).
  • In flight, air is a fluid, so the wings of planes are designed to create a pressure difference that provide for some lift (an upwards net force).
  • With a lower pressure above the wing, and a higher pressure underneath, the resultant force is upwards (↑).
