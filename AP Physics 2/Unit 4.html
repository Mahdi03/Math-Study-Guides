• The mirrors we see on a day-to-day basis are flat mirrors that usually show us a virtual and upright "image" of ourselves
  • In both mirrors and lenses, curved in both concave and convex directions, an upright image means that it's a virtual image, and vice versa, an inverted image means it's real
  • A converging lens is just a convex lens, and a diverging lens is a convex lens.
  • Real images can only form with concave mirrors and convex (converging) lens, but that doesn't mean they always do. However, convex mirrors and concave (diverging) lens always produce virtual images.
