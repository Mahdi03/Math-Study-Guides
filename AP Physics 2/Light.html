Light Models:

  • Light is just one form of electromagnetic radiation.
  • Light can be modeled in three ways:
    1. The ray model: a laser beam
    2. The wave model: ripples
    3. The particle model: collisions with other particles
  • The ray model and the particle model are both linear types of models, and the intricacy of quantum relativity allows for all three models to hold true in certain situations.
  • Sound also travels outwards in waves
  • When light is shone through a small slit, a pattern is formed from overlapping waves:
    • When the waves add together, it is constructive interference
    • When the waves cancel each other out, it is destructive interference
