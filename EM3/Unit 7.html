General Information:

    • The polar form for a point is (r, θ), where "r" represents the distance away from the origin and θ represents the angle from the x-axis
    • To convert a point from Cartesian coordinates to Polar coordinates, use r = x2 + y2 and θ = tan−1(

    • When converting from Cartesian to Polar coordinates, your calculator can give you the wrong value of tan−1 because of the Quadrant. To fix that use this table to show what you have to do to the angle to get the right value of θ:
      Quadrant Value of tan−1:
      I Use the calculator value
      II Add 180° or ℼ to the calculator value
      III Add 180° or ℼ to the calculator value
      IV Add 360° or 2ℼ to the calculator value

    • To convert a point from Polar coordinates to Cartesian coordinates, use x = r θ and y = r θ
    De Moivre's Theorem:
  • Just like complex numbers in the Cartesian plane, there are also complex numbers in the Polar Plane. These are denoted as such:
    z = r(θ + iθ)

    • Polar Graphs:

  • They come in many shapes and sizes
    • Circles:
  • These can come in three ways:
    • r = a centered at origin a = radius 0 ≤ θ ≤ 2π
    r = a θ centered on the x-axis a = diameter 0 ≤ θ ≤ π
    r = a θ centered on the y-axis

    Cardioids:
  • These come in two forms:
    • r = a ± a θ Symmetry with x-axis 0 ≤ θ ≤ 2π
    r = a ± a θ Symmetry with y-axis
  • If it's r = a + a θ then most of the graph will be on the positive side, if it's r = a − a θ , then most of the graph will be on the negative side.
  • The point always is on the origin.
    • r = 4 + 4 θ r = 4 − 4 θ
    r = 4 + 4 θ r = 4 − 4 θ
    Limaçons:
  • These come in the forms:
    • r = a ±b θ Symmetry with x-axis 0 ≤ θ ≤ 2π
    r = a ± b θ Symmetry with y-axis
  • If a > b, then there is no loop, rather a dimple that is never on the origin.
  • If a < b, then there is an inner loop.
    • r = 5 + 3 θ r = 3 − 5 θ
    r = 3 + 5 θ r = 5 − 3 θ
    Flower Functions:
  • These come in the form:
    • r = a nθ
    r = a nθ
  • If n is odd, then there are n pedals and the domain is 0 ≤ θ ≤ π. If n is even, then there are 2n pedals and the domain is 0 ≤ θ ≤ 2π.
